\section{Introduction}
The increasing volume of scientific literature necessitates advanced tools to assist researchers in drafting and refining their work. Automated writing systems and tools for text revision have emerged as promising solutions, aiming to improve the efficiency and quality of academic writing \cite{smith2020automated, jones2021text}. However, integrating various capabilities, such as content generation, stylistic improvement, and adherence to structural conventions, into a cohesive workflow remains a challenge.

This paper demonstrates the core structure and components of a conceptual "paper improver pipeline," designed to automatically enhance draft manuscripts. While the full implementation and evaluation are beyond the scope of this short paper, we outline the basic framework. We follow a modular approach, inspired by previous work in automated document processing \cite{brown2019modular}.

This figure serves to illustrate how visual elements would be integrated into the document structure produced by the pipeline.

\section{Conclusion}
This short paper presented the foundational structure for a conceptual paper improver pipeline, highlighting how automated systems can be designed to assist in academic writing. We demonstrated the basic document structure and the inclusion of visual elements via a placeholder figure. While this work is preliminary, it lays the groundwork for developing more sophisticated automated tools to support researchers in producing high-quality manuscripts. Future work will focus on implementing the various modules of the pipeline and evaluating its effectiveness on real-world academic texts.
